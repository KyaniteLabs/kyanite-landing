Proof

Authority has to come from real work.

No fake logos. No inflated claims. No hand-wavy transformation story. This is the kind of work Kyanite has already done on a live VPS and AI-operator stack.

1

Security and exposure tightened

A live KVM4 stack was audited and hardened in place: secret-file permissions corrected, public/private routes clarified, and direct host binds pushed to loopback where appropriate.

  • Paperclip moved behind authentication and kept noindex.
  • Landing stayed public/indexable.
  • R2R stayed public but non-indexed until launch readiness.
2

Governance made legible

The stack's source-of-truth problem was mapped and normalized so future edits stop hitting the wrong paths.

  • Source-of-truth registry added for services and live paths.
  • Stale cron assumptions pruned.
  • Restore drill and backup verification added.
3

Operator leverage improved

The AI operator layer was made more useful and less reckless: runtime tuned, high-authority Docker actions narrowed behind a guard, and revenue workflows turned into something the system can actually use.

  • High-authority Docker actions reduced behind an allowlist.
  • Revenue OS added for offers, proof, and action ranking.
  • Gateway and cron status drift fixed so operations are legible again.
